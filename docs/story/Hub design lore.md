

# Pillars for Hub Design

## Core Philosophy
The design shifts from simple environmental storytelling to **psychological storytelling**. This approach aligns perfectly with the **fallen god narrative**, where the hub serves as a manifestation of denial and self-deception.

### The Three Pillars
* **Pillar 1:** Isolation (False Restoration → Revelation).
* **Pillar 2:** Sanctuary (Ruin → Truth).
* ]**Pillar 3:** Perception vs. Reality.

---

## Hub Evolution: First Touch
**Option A: "Pocket Dimension That Was Always Real"** 

* **Early Game:** The hub is foggy and void-like with undefined edges. You cannot see beyond its borders.
* **Later Game:** Fog clears and walls collapse. You see the city around it and can physically exit the space.

### Progression Phases
| Phase | Visual Presentation | Player Perception | Reality |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Phase 1: The Illusion** (Start) | Warm, lively, soft lighting with intact buildings and calm music. | The town is struggling but savable. | It is already destroyed, but the reality is hidden. |
| **Phase 2: Restoration** (Mid-Game) | Colors saturate, flowers appear, and buildings repair. NPCs appear happier. | Player sprite gains a glow and ornamentation. | Small cracks begin: NPC dialogue becomes strange and background/shadows flicker. |
| **Phase 3: Revelation** (Late Game) | Lighting changes, music stops, and color drains as the illusion collapses. | You return to find the hub is physically in ruins. | It always was ruins. This is your "Roundtable" moment. |

---

## Visual & Technical Design
### Example Strong Concept
* **Layout:** A circular stone sanctuary surrounded by darkness.
* **Center:** A central broken monument.
* **Structure:** Four sealed archways leading to future dungeons.
* **Reveal:** The background is initially a black void, later revealed to be high above a buried ancient city.

### Scope & Constraints
* **Keep it Realistic:** Don't build large explorable hubs or complex verticality.
* **Technical Implementation:** Use 1 or 2 screens max.
* **Visual Change:** Achieve transformations via tile swaps, lighting, and background changes rather than heavy scripting.

## Characters & Themes
### The Girl’s Evolution
The girl is not evil, but remorseful; however, she is actively manipulating perception.

| Artifact Count | Girl Sprite | Emotional Tone |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0** | Mortal, chained | Quiet  |
| **2** | Slight glow | Hopeful  |
| **4** | Divine form (may be unstable/glitching) | Confident |
| **Final** | Cracked / fractured | Desperate |

### Thematic Core
The story centers on **denial, guilt, and the refusal to accept consequences**. 
* **Gameplay Symmetry:** Artifacts give the player abilities, so every time the player becomes stronger, the illusion becomes stronger. 
* **Complicity:** The player is complicit in the god's pretense of restoring the town.
