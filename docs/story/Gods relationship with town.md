# Lore Concept: The Weaver of the Hearth

## Core Narrative
The story follows a **fallen god** in human form who guides a hero to recover her lost artifacts. While the hero believes they are on a noble quest to restore a ruined sanctuary, they are actually being used as a biological vessel to regather her divinity and fuel a grand illusion.

---

## 1. The Fallen God: The Weaver
* **Domain:** Formerly the Goddess of Community, Craft, and the Social Fabric.
* **The Betrayal:** Her "children" (the townsfolk) sacrificed her to survive, "shackling" her divinity into artifacts and "unraveling" her.
* **The Transformation:** She no longer nurtures individuals; she views people as materials and "threads" to be manipulated for revenge.
* **The Artifacts:** These are literally pieces of her **Divine Anatomy** (eyes, heart, hands) that were turned into tools by the townspeople.

---

## 2. The Illusion of the Town
The "safe-place" hub is an illusion built from memory and spite to trick the hero into continuing his journey.

* **Guilt-Puppets:** The NPCs are the literal souls of the original townspeople, leashed into a "prospering" reality governed by her rules.
* **The Uncanny Valley:** The illusion is imperfect. NPCs may repeat dialogue or "glitch," such as a tavernkeeper laughing for ten minutes until a visible thread "resets" him.
* **Visual Cracks:** As the hero gains power, he may notice children’s shadows are actually tethers leading back to the God's house.

---

## 3. The Hero’s Dehumanization
Every artifact the hero retrieves replaces a piece of his humanity, a process he perceives as "Divine Evolution" due to the god's influence.

| Artifact | Hero's Perception | Horrific Reality |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Gilded Sinew** | Increased strength and agility. | Muscle fibers are replaced by golden wire that tugs at bones like a marionette. |
| **The All-Seeing Loom** | Tactical HUD or "Foresight". | Eyes become hollow pits of spinning thread; he sees only vulnerability points. |
| **The Sovereign Heart** | Immunity to fear and pain. | Heart is replaced by cold stone clockwork; he loses all empathy. |

---

## 4. Interaction with the Outside World
While the hero sees himself as a "shining knight," the rest of the world sees the truth.
* **The Aura:** People avoid or fear the hero because he looks like a "walking corpse" or a "tear in reality".
* **The Harvest:** Local reality wilts around the hero—flowers die and animals flee—because he is unknowingly "harvesting" the environment to feed the hub's illusion.
* **The Mirror:** If the hero sees his reflection in an uninfluenced mirror, he sees a hollowed-out husk filled with jagged, glowing artifacts.

---

## 5. The Final Revelation & Boss Fight
The Weaver serves as the final boss, using the hero's progress against him.
* **Weaponized NPCs:** She wields the NPCs the hero befriended; the blacksmith becomes a weapon, and the healer negates the hero’s damage.
* **The Harvest Mechanic:** During the fight, she can "deactivate" or pull on the artifacts inside the hero, forcing him to strike himself with his own limbs.
* **The Final Choice:** To defeat her, the hero must destroy the artifacts, which effectively means destroying himself as there is almost no "human" left.
